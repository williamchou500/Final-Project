1. What have you done so far?

First, we tried several different regressions to predict the glucose levels from the chosen dataset. After trial and error, we chose Random Forest regression which showed the l most accurate predictions out of all regressions we tried. We used Random Forest Classifier to determine the magnitude of each nutrient value present in the dataset: calories, total carbs, dietary fiber, sugar, total fat, protein as well as time, Hb1Ac, and gender, to predict the amount glucose levels would increase within 2 hours after eating. We used this to make a tool where you can input nutrition information and other data to get a predicted glucose level after eating. We get these predictions by adding the predicted increase in glucose after 2 hours to resting glucose levels (currently set to 110). These results are logged and graphed, additionally, a weekly summary is made available after 7 days/logs. The graph shows a spike of glucose corresponding to the meal consumed. The face identifying whether the spike occurred was added to be replaced by a different animation later.
